# Welcome to Ryn's "Officially Unofficial" Bombe Guide

## What is Bombe?

Bombe[^1] is a Minecraft-like automation puzzle game developed and published by Charlie Brej in 2023.  The basis of the game is to create various rules to infer which cells contain bombs or are empty; as described by [the game's Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2262930/Bombe/):

> Minesweeper, but you only solve each situation once.

> - Regions (number bubbles connected by lines) generated from clues on the board
> - Rules act on regions to mark bombs, clear squares or create new regions
> - A checker tells you if your rule is formally correct
> - Rules are auto applied forever after, resolving the situations you have solved before
> - Only the hard situations remain.
> - A hint system can mark the determinable squares and remove all unnecessary regions from the board.
> - Regions can be hidden or discarded using rules
> - Thousands of levels to play, all solvable without guessing.

## What is this guide?

I often find myself quite... well, obsessed with this game.  Am I good at it?  By no means!  But do I love it?  Definitely!

In my struggles to make progress in the game, I found a noticable lack of comprehensive guides on it.  There are a couple guides on Steam (a beginners guide[^2] and a guide specifically on priorities[^3]), two excellence documents on rules and theory by chao[^4], and a Discord server[^5].  What I didn't find was something that bridged the gap (or at least in a way that made sense to me).  And yet, these resources inspired me - if I am struggling to find what I want, maybe I should create it.

That is what I am ultimately setting out to make: a comprehensive guide on the game and my time with it.  I see this as my walkthrough of how I went about solving the puzzles, my theory analysis around the rules, and a sort of journal of my journey through all of this.  And I hope that my little obsession may be of help to someone who finds them in the same situation I was in.  After all, let's "solve each situation once" (spoliers: I restart this game _a lot_).
